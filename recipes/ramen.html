Ramen

Description:

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish featuring Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a flavorful broth. The broth can be made from various ingredients, such as pork, chicken, or miso, often simmered for hours to develop rich flavors. Common toppings include sliced pork (chashu), green onions, nori (seaweed), and soft-boiled eggs. To make ramen, you typically start by preparing the broth, cooking the noodles separately until they're just tender, and then assembling the dish by adding the noodles to the broth and topping it with your choice of garnishes. Each region in Japan has its own variations, making ramen a versatile and beloved comfort food!

Ingredients:

  • Pork or chicken bones
  • Water
  • Soy sauce (for shoyu ramen)
  • Miso paste (for miso ramen)
  • Salt (for shio ramen)
  • Aromatics (garlic, ginger, onion)
  • Kombu (dried kelp) orbonito flakes for umami

    • To make the noodles:

    • Wheat flour
    • Water
    • Kansui (alkaline mineral water, for texture)

    Extras/Toppings:

    • Chashu (braised pork belly)
    • Soft-boiled eggs
    • Scallions
    • Nori
    • Bamboo shoots
    • Corn
    • Bean sprouts
    • Sesame seeds

    How to make:

    1. Boil water and cook noodles
    2. Prepare broth and add vegetables
    3. Cook protein and combine with broth
    4. Season and garnish
